Project Three

Caspar David Friedrich, who moved away from the church and lived only in the 19th century German romantic art, which can only be linked to the level of sacredness, lived shortly after the birth of great ideas such as realism and individualism. and his art is considered to be beyond his own age. László Beke sums up his ars poetica strikingly: “he created a world of colors with a unique atmosphere, namely in a new conception of landscape, which can be called both visionary and realistic, and both realistic and allegorical or symbolic. In Friedrich’s landscapes, almost every detail is credibly self-evident, and at the same time something beyond itself, according to a new conception of nature that evokes the same universal spiritual force or law in the phenomena of nature as in man who is one with them. And the painter, himself a part of this spiritual unity, submits himself to the forces of nature, and on the other hand, when he paints them, he projects his own spiritual state into them. " Friedrich’s ars poetics can be found in landscapes, everything that the artist has and what he thought of the world is in the picture.
